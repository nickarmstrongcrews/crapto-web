N4R FAQ

What is NFTs for Refugees (N4R)?

Unlike other charitable organizations, we believe in looking beyond the *immediate* needs of refugees to consider their *long-term* welfare. We give them NFTs to set them up to succeed in the growing digital economy of tomorrow, striking a hearty blow against the spectre of generational poverty.

What is an NFT?

Our NFTs are "non-food tokens," which work exactly the same way as the more common "non-fungible token" NFTs (see Wikipedia article).

How do the NFTs get to the refugees?

Any refugee may enter the refugee portal and access their NFT.

Can I donate existing external NFTs?

Not currently; we are working on this feature and hope it will be available soon.

Can I export my NFT outside of this site?

No, your NFT is not for you; it is for refugees. If you are a donor *and* a refugee, you may access your NFT through the refugee portal.

How do I get an NFT to donate?

You can generate and purchase a new NFT in exchange for Crapto currency.

How do I get Crapto currency?

The Donate page gives the option to create an empty Crapto wallet and mine some Crapto. It should take roughly 1 second to mine enough to buy an NFT.

Is this NFT worth anything?

There is no inherent value in our NFTs (just like any other NFT). However, there may be demand, and anything in demand has value in some sense. One external NFT sold for US$70 million.

Are NFTs and cryptocurrency related?

They are both based on blockchain technology to verify transactions. Additionally, many NFTs may be bought (or sold) in exchange for cryptocurrency. One key difference is that if you have a Bitcoin and I have a Bitcoin and we swap them, we have the same thing we started with; i.e., they are "fungible." But if you have a son and I have a son and we swap them, much has changed; i.e., sons (like NFTs) are non-fungible.

Are all the NFTs you generate unique?

Yes, that is what makes an NFT "non-fungible." Our NFTs may appear similar to the naked eye, but each has subtle differences that make them uniquer than a Millennial snowflake.

Am I missing something? Is an NFT just an image?

Like most things on the internet, a general abstract concept tends to get used primarily in one specific way and then people come to equate that specific thing with the entirety of the original abstract concept (e.g., the meme). An NFT is not an image; it is a proof-of-ownership (like a deed to a house). While in principle anything can be "owned," NFTs are most often used these days to prove ownership of computer-generated images.

So you're not even delivering the images themselves to refugees, but only tokens representing ownership of the images?

Bingo. I hope this helps to clarify the precise value of all the NFTs you read about in the news.

While you can participate in verification (and thereby mine Crapto) right from the Crapto web interface, you can also add your server node to our network by downloading and building the source code on the Crapto FAQ.
